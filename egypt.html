Amun

Known also as Amen, Amun, Ammon and later Amun-Ra.
Rose to prominance in Middle and New kingdoms. Was eventualy combined with Ra , the ruling diety of the Old kingdom to become the King of Gods and Ruler of the Great Ennead. His name means "Hidden one, Mysterious of Form" Beside his human form he is also represented as a ram or goose.
His eaqule in other mythology: Zeus , Jupiter
Son: Khonsus , Wife: Mut

Mut

She is the Vulture goddess, often depicted as a woman with a colorful dress and vulture headdress. She is also shown with a lion-head in her more aggressive aspect. Mut played the role of devine mother, along with Hathor and Isis. In some depictings she is often confused with Isis.
Father: Ra, Husband: Amun, Son: Khonsu.

Khonsus

Known also as Khnos, Khensu, Khuns.
Together with his parents he formed the Theban triad. He is the moon god, depicted as a man with a falcon head, crescent moon headdress and the full lunar disc. He is sometimes represented by a baboon, along with Thoth, who is also a lunar deity. He was belived to drive out evil spirits. His eaqule in other mythology: Selene, Luna
Father: Amun, Mother: Mut

Nut

She is usually depicted in her human form, with an elongated body symbolizing the sky. Nut swollows the setting son, Ra, each evening and then gives birth to him every morning. Childeren: Isis, Osiris, Seth, Nephythys.

Shu

He and his wife were the first gods created by Atum. He was the god of dry air and sunlight. He is usually depicted as a man with a plume headdress. His function was to hold up the body of Nut
Wife: Tefnut, Children: Nut, Geb
