EGYPT

Amun

Known also as Amen, Amun, Ammon and later Amun-Ra.
Rose to prominance in Middle and New kingdoms. Was eventualy combined with Ra , the ruling diety of the Old kingdom to become the King of Gods and Ruler of the Great Ennead. His name means "Hidden one, Mysterious of Form" Beside his human form he is also represented as a ram or goose.
His eaqule in other mythology: Zeus , Jupiter
Son: Khonsus , Wife: Mut

Mut

She is the Vulture goddess, often depicted as a woman with a colorful dress and vulture headdress. She is also shown with a lion-head in her more aggressive aspect. Mut played the role of devine mother, along with Hathor and Isis. In some depictings she is often confused with Isis.
Father: Ra, Husband: Amun, Son: Khonsu.

Khonsus

Known also as Khnos, Khensu, Khuns.
Together with his parents he formed the Theban triad. He is the moon god, depicted as a man with a falcon head, crescent moon headdress and the full lunar disc. He is sometimes represented by a baboon, along with Thoth, who is also a lunar deity. He was belived to drive out evil spirits. His eaqule in other mythology: Selene, Luna
Father: Amun, Mother: Mut

Nut

She is usually depicted in her human form, with an elongated body symbolizing the sky. Nut swollows the setting son, Ra, each evening and then gives birth to him every morning. Childeren: Isis, Osiris, Seth, Nephythys.

Shu

He and his wife were the first gods created by Atum. He was the god of dry air and sunlight. He is usually depicted as a man with a plume headdress. His function was to hold up the body of Nut
Wife: Tefnut, Children: Nut, Geb

Geb

An Earth god. Associated with fertality. He imprisons the dead in his body.
Childeren: Isis, Osiris, Seth, Nephythys.

Anubis

Protector of the dead. He is depicted with a jackals head.
Father: #Seth, Mother: Nephythys.

Bastet

Depicted as a woman with a cats head, or a cat. Origanlly a vengeful lioness she evolved into a goddess of pleasure.

Bes

Is depicted from the front, as a dwarf with his tounge stuck out. He is assosiated with entertainment and fun, childbirth, and a guardian.

Hapi

He is the god of inundation in the Nile. He is represented as a pot bellied man with breasts and river plants.

Hathor

Patron goddess of women, beauty, love, pleasure and music. She is depicted as a cow, a woman with cow ears or with a cow horn headdress. She also has a darker side, she was sent to punish mankind for its wickedness, during which she slaughtered large numbers of people.
Father: Ra, Husband: Horus.

Horus

Enemy of the god Seth. Depicted as a man or a hawk. Sometimes he is shown as a child on his mothers lap. The god of the sky andd the protector of kings. Eventually Horus defeted Seth and became the king of Egypt.
Parents: Isis and Osiris, Wife: Hathor

Isis

She is assosiated with funeral rites and is said to have made the first mummy of Osiris. The giver of life, a healer and protector. She is reoresented with a throne on her head. When depicted with the young Horus, she is know as the Mother of God. She represents the ideal wife and mother, loving, caring and devoted.
Husband: Osiris, Son: Horus

Khepre

Known also as Khepri, Khepra, Khepera or Khepre.
Is a creator god, depicted zs a Scarab or a mand with a Scara head. One of the first gods, self created. His name means - he who has come into being. It is thought that he rolled the sun across the sky.

Khumn

Depicted as a ram headed man. God of the cataracts, a potter and creator god who guards the source of the Nile.

Ma'at

The goddess of truth and justice. She embodies the essential harmony of the universe. She is depicted wearing an ostrich feather. Her power regulztes the seasons, the movement of starts. The symbol of ethics. The ultimate judge in the afterlife. The heart of the deceased was weight against her feather. Ammut, s deamon, devourer of the dead ate those who failed.

Montu

A warrior god. Later pushed to the sidelines by Amun. He then took on the true attributes of a war god.

Nephythys

Known as Lady of the Mansion or Palace. She was disgusted by Seth's murder of Osiris and helped Isis against him. Together with her they are the protectors of the dead
Parents: Geb and Nut, Husband: Seth, Sister: Isis, Son: Anubis

Osiris

Origanlly a vegetation god linked with crops. The mythological first king of Egypt. One of the head gods. Murdured by Seth. Brought back to life by Isis. He then became the ruler of the underworld and judge of the dead. He is usually depicted as a mummy holding a crook and flail of kingship. He has blue, black or green skin.
Brother: Seth, Wife: Isis

Ptah

A creator god. Said to have made the world from the thoughts in his heart and words. Depicted as a mummy with hands coming from his wrappings, holding a staff. Assosiated with craftsmen.

Ra

Known also as Re.
The supreme god, represented as a man with the head of a hawk. However, when he passes through the underworld each day, he is depicted with a ram head. Each day he travels across the sky in the form of the sun, riding in his solar boat and then he traveles the underworld, where he defeats the allies of chaos. He is reborn each morning. Many gods were so influenced by him, they fused with him later on.

Sobek

A crocodile god. Depicted as a man with a crocodile head.

Seth

Known also as Set, Setekh, Suty and Sutekh..
The god of chaos, darkness, confusion and is represented by a man with a unknown head. He murdured his brother and took the throne of Egypt. He is the evil in the eternal struggle between good and evil. He sometimes accompanies Ra, causing storms and bad weather.
His eaqule in other mythology: Zeus, Jupiter
Parents: Geb and Nut, Sister: Isis, Son: Anubis

Tefnut

She and Shu were the first gods created by Atum. She is the goddess of dampness. Depicted as a woman with a lions head or a lioness.
Children: Geb and Nut, Husband: Shu

Thoth

The god of writing and knowladge. Depicted as a man with an ibises head, holding a scribe and palette, or as a baboon. He is as old as the oldest gods, assosiated with the moon. He records the deedes of the dead on their day of judgement into the Book of the Dead.
Simmilar to: Greece: Hermes
